# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

A checklist for responsible and professional social media use during your college years.

---

## ✅ DO's

| # | Do |
|---|-----|
| 1 | **Think before you post** – Ask yourself if the content is something you'd be comfortable with a future employer, professor, or family member seeing. |
| 2 | **Keep your profiles professional** – Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and maintain a bio that reflects your academic and career interests. |
| 3 | **Protect your privacy** – Regularly review your privacy settings and limit personal information (address, phone number, daily schedule) visible to the public. |
| 4 | **Give credit where it's due** – Always credit original creators, authors, or sources when sharing or reposting content. |
| 5 | **Use social media to network positively** – Connect with professors, alumni, and professionals in your field; engage in meaningful academic and career-related discussions. |
| 6 | **Report cyberbullying or harmful content** – Use platform reporting tools if you encounter harassment, hate speech, or dangerous misinformation. |
| 7 | **Take regular digital breaks** – Schedule offline time to protect your mental health and maintain a healthy balance between online and real-world activities. |

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

| # | Don't |
|---|-------|
| 1 | **Don't share confidential or sensitive information** – Avoid posting exam content, personal details of classmates, or any information shared in confidence by your institution. |
| 2 | **Don't engage in cyberbullying or online harassment** – Never post hateful, discriminatory, or demeaning content about peers, faculty, or any individual or group. |
| 3 | **Don't overshare your location or daily routine** – Avoid real-time check-ins or posts that reveal your exact whereabouts, as this can be a safety risk. |
| 4 | **Don't post under the influence** – Avoid posting impulsively when emotional, tired, or under the influence — once something is online, it can be difficult to fully erase. |
| 5 | **Don't spread misinformation** – Always verify information from credible sources before sharing news, statistics, or health-related content. |
| 6 | **Don't neglect academic responsibilities for social media** – Avoid excessive scrolling or posting during class, study time, or when deadlines are approaching. |
| 7 | **Don't engage in or share plagiarized content** – Posting or sharing someone else's work as your own violates academic integrity and copyright rules. |
