# Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India | Digital Ambassador Initiative

---

## ✅ General Online Safety

- [ ] **1. Use strong, unique passwords** for every account. Use a passphrase like `Mango$Rain#2026` instead of `password123`. Never reuse passwords across platforms.

- [ ] **2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts — email, social media, banking apps. Even if your password is stolen, 2FA blocks attackers.

- [ ] **3. Never click links in unsolicited SMS, WhatsApp, or email messages** — even if they appear to come from HDFC Bank, Amazon, or "TRAI." Go directly to the official website instead.

- [ ] **4. Verify before you trust.** If someone calls claiming to be from a bank, police, or government department asking for OTPs or personal details — hang up and call the official number yourself.

- [ ] **5. Keep your apps and phone OS updated.** Security patches fix vulnerabilities that hackers actively exploit. Enable auto-updates.

- [ ] **6. Use only trusted, secure Wi-Fi.** Avoid logging into banking or email on public/café Wi-Fi. Use your mobile data or a VPN for sensitive tasks.

- [ ] **7. Review app permissions regularly.** A flashlight app does not need access to your contacts or location. Revoke unnecessary permissions in Settings.

- [ ] **8. Be careful what you post publicly.** Your date of birth, phone number, hometown, and college name — combined — can be used for identity theft or targeted scams.

---

## 💰 Financial & UPI Safety (Mandatory Focus)

- [ ] **9. Remember the Golden Rule of UPI: You NEVER enter your PIN to RECEIVE money.** Entering your UPI PIN always authorises a payment FROM your account. If someone asks you to "accept" money by entering your PIN — it is a scam. Stop immediately.

- [ ] **10. Never share OTPs, UPI PINs, CVV numbers, or card details with anyone** — not even someone claiming to be your bank's customer care. Banks never ask for these over call or chat.

- [ ] **11. Set transaction limits on your UPI apps** (Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm). Most apps allow you to cap daily transfer limits — use this as a safety buffer.

- [ ] **12. Always double-check the recipient's UPI ID or QR code** before confirming any payment. Fraudsters use IDs that look similar to legitimate businesses (e.g., `amazon.pay.help@upi` instead of `amazonpay@apl`).

---

## 🚨 If You Become a Victim — Act Immediately

1. **Call Helpline 1930** (National Cyber Crime Helpline — available 24×7) to report financial fraud and request a transaction hold.
2. **File a report online at:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) — the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal by the Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India.
3. Inform your bank immediately to block your card/UPI and dispute the transaction.
4. Preserve all evidence: screenshots, call logs, transaction IDs.
